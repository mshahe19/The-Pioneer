FTSE and European stocks tumble after fake US inflation report

European markets slumped in a sea of red on Wednesday after a fake US inflation report that circulated on Tuesday hit Wall Street stocks. In London, the FTSE 100 tumbled 0.8% after the opening bell, the CAC was 0.5% lower in Paris and the DAX fell 1.1% in Frankfurt. It comes after the UK economy grew by more than expected in May, with a bounce back across all sectors despite the cost of living crisis. Gross domestic product (GDP) expanded 0.5% from April when output declined 0.3%, according to the Office for National Statistics (ONS). Economists polled by Reuters had predicted the economy to stagnate.

The ONS said health was the main contributor, with services output up 0.4% as human health and social work activities grew by 2.1%. A slight uptick had been anticipated, but at 0.5% the pace of growth has caught many by surprise. These figures represent just one month – albeit a crucial one because it means the quarter as a whole doesn’t meet the criteria for negative growth – but one month can never tell the whole story.
